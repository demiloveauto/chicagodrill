Key Artists

Chicago Drill has produced several influential rappers who shaped the genre and inspired others.

Chief Keef is often credited as the pioneer, while Lil Durk and King Von brought new styles and stories to the scene.

Chief Keef

Known for "I Don't Like" and starting the Drill movement.

Brief bio: Chief Keef (Keith Cozart) rose from Chicago's South Side as a teenage phenom. His raw delivery and minimalist production helped define early Drill.

Notable releases: Finally Rich (mixtape), "I Don't Like" (single).

Lil Durk

Blends melodic rap with Drill roots.

Brief bio: Lil Durk (Durk Banks) combined street-oriented Drill with melodic hooks, expanding the genre's emotional range.

Notable releases: Remember My Name (album), "3 Headed Goat" (single appearance).

King Von

Famous for storytelling in his lyrics.

Brief bio: King Von (Dayvon Bennett) was known for vivid storytelling and narrative-driven tracks that painted scenes from his life and neighborhood.

Notable releases: Welcome to O'Block (album), "Crazy Story" (single).

Drill's Influence

  1. Changed the sound of modern rap, making it raw and authentic.
  2. Inspired Drill scenes in cities like New York and London.
  3. Many artists use Drill beats and flows today.
